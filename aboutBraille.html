About Braille


According to the American Foundation for the Blind,
"Braille is defined as a system of raised dots that can be read with the fingers by people who are blind or have low vision."

Braille is a code that can be used by many languages such as English, Spanish, Chinese and many others.

The code or symbols are formed within a space that contains six dots placed in two parallel lines each with three dots.
