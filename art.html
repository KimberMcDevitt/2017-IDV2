Braille and Feel-Good Relief in Art


The Paul Klee museum in Bern, Switzerland offers visually impared museum patrons an opportunity to participate with the art by providing feel-good reliefs of ceratin exhibitions. This is a technique used to describe the painting description in braille but then the painting in front was tactile and in the same shape of the painting. This type of display enables vision impared patrions the ability to paricipate fully in the art experience.
